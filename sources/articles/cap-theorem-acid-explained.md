# Apache Cassandra Internals — CAP Theorem and Distributed Trade-offs

Classic Habr article (267K views) explaining Apache Cassandra internals as a practical lens into distributed systems fundamentals. Covers consistent hashing, replication strategies, tunable consistency levels, and the CAP theorem trade-offs embodied in Cassandra's design. Demonstrates how ACID properties map (or don't) to eventually-consistent systems and introduces the BASE model as an alternative.

## Key Topics

- CAP theorem and its practical implications
- ACID vs BASE trade-offs
- Consistent hashing and data distribution
- Tunable consistency (read/write quorums)
- Replication strategies and failure handling
- Eventual consistency and conflict resolution
- Distributed systems design patterns
